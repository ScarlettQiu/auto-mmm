06 — Data Limitations

Three structural issues, honestly stated

No amount of model tuning will resolve these. They require better data.

⚠️
August 2015 Anomaly
GMV = INR 419,196 in a dataset where every other month exceeds INR 176M. Almost certainly a data entry error. It inflates train MAPE to 310–877% and distorts all model diagnostics. Fixing this is the single highest-leverage action available.
📏
Sample Size: 10 observations for 8 channels
10 training observations cannot support 8 channel predictors simultaneously. Zero ROI on 6 channels means unmeasurable — not ineffective. Weekly data for 12+ months (52+ obs) is needed to support all channels concurrently.
🔗
Collinearity: Channels co-spent in the same months
Affiliates, Sponsorship, and Online.marketing all spent heavily in Sep/Nov 2015 and Mar 2016. Monthly data cannot disentangle their individual contributions. Weekly or daily data with more natural variation would help separate them.
07 — Recommendations

Three actions, in priority order

1
Protect Affiliates budget
Three independent rounds, two model types, consistent positive ROI (0.75–0.90 INR per INR 1 spent). This is the strongest finding the current dataset can support. Do not reduce Affiliates spend pending a better-powered analysis.
2
Investigate the August 2015 data point first
INR 419,196 GMV is almost certainly a data entry error — missing zeros or wrong month assignment. Correcting it could unlock measurable signal for the 6 currently zero-ROI channels. Check source systems against the raw transaction log.
3
Collect weekly data for 12+ months before re-running
Monthly data gives 10 training observations — statistically underpowered for 8 channels. Weekly data gives 52+ observations per year, enough to support all channels simultaneously with proper uncertainty bounds. Aim for 18 months of weekly data.
Bottom line: Affiliates has now shown consistent positive ROI across three independent rounds and two model types — this is the strongest finding the current dataset can support. For all other channels, do not make budget decisions based on this analysis.
